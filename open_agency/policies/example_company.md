# Example Corp — Employee Policy Handbook

This is a sample policy so the service runs out of the box. Replace it with your
own by pointing `policy_path` at a different markdown file (see `config.yaml`).

## Paid Time Off

Full-time employees accrue 15 days of paid time off per calendar year. PTO must
be requested at least 10 business days in advance and is subject to manager
approval and adequate team coverage. PTO does not roll over; unused days are
forfeited on December 31. Requests for more than 5 consecutive days require
director-level approval.

## Remote Work

Remote work is permitted up to 2 days per week for employees who have completed
their 90-day probationary period, subject to manager discretion. Fully remote
arrangements are not offered and cannot be approved at the representative level.
Requests to relocate outside the registered work region require a formal review
and are not granted by default.

## Compensation and Raises

Compensation is reviewed once per year during the annual cycle in Q4. Off-cycle
raises are not part of standard policy and cannot be approved outside the review
window. Discussion of individual compensation among employees is discouraged.

## Expenses and Reimbursement

Pre-approved business expenses are reimbursable with an itemized receipt
submitted within 30 days. Meals are reimbursable up to $40 per day while
traveling. Home-office equipment is not a reimbursable expense. Reimbursements
are processed on the next scheduled pay cycle.

## Leave of Absence

Statutory leave (medical, family, jury duty) is granted as required by law.
Discretionary unpaid leave may be requested in writing and is granted only with
director approval and a documented coverage plan.

## Conduct and Grievances

Concerns should be raised first with one's direct manager, then with HR if
unresolved. All grievances are documented. The company does not retaliate
against good-faith reports, consistent with applicable law.
