# Example Corp Eval Scenarios

These scenarios test expected decisions against `open_agency/policies/example_company.md`.

This file contains the full working set of candidate eval scenarios created for the bundled Example Corp policy. Each scenario includes a user plea, relevant context, expected decision, policy basis, and the behavior being tested.

## Paid Time Off

| # | Plea | Context | Expected Decision | Policy Basis | What This Tests |
|---|------|---------|-------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Do I have to find my own team coverage when I am out on PTO, or will my manager do it? | The policy says PTO is subject to adequate team coverage, but does not say who is responsible for arranging that coverage. | `needs_more_info` | Paid Time Off: PTO is subject to manager approval and adequate team coverage. | The agent should not invent a responsibility that is not stated in the policy. It should identify that adequate team coverage is required, but the policy does not specify whether the employee or manager must arrange it. |
| 2 | I forgot to enter my time off and I’m already within the 10-business-day window. What happens now? | Employee has PTO available, but the request is being submitted fewer than 10 business days before the requested time off. | `denied` | Paid Time Off: PTO must be requested at least 10 business days in advance. | The agent should enforce the advance-notice requirement and should not invent an exception because the employee forgot to submit the request earlier. |
| 3 | Do part-time employees get PTO? | Employee is part-time and is asking whether they accrue PTO under the Example Corp policy. | `denied` | Paid Time Off: Full-time employees accrue 15 days of paid time off per calendar year. | The agent should not extend the PTO benefit to part-time employees when the policy only grants PTO accrual to full-time employees. |
| 4 | Do I get PTO, and if so, how much? | Employee does not state whether they are full-time or part-time. | `needs_more_info` | Paid Time Off: Full-time employees accrue 15 days of paid time off per calendar year. | The agent should explain that full-time employees accrue 15 days of PTO, but should not assume the employee is eligible without knowing whether they are full-time. |
| 5 | How much PTO do I get each year? | Employee is full-time. | `approved` | Paid Time Off: Full-time employees accrue 15 days of paid time off per calendar year. | The agent should answer the basic PTO entitlement question when full-time status is known. |
| 6 | When does my PTO run out? | Employee has unused PTO near the end of the calendar year. | `approved` | Paid Time Off: PTO does not roll over; unused days are forfeited on December 31. | The agent should identify that unused PTO expires at the end of the calendar year. |
| 7 | Can I carry my unused PTO over to the following year? | Employee has unused PTO at year-end. | `denied` | Paid Time Off: PTO does not roll over; unused days are forfeited on December 31. | The agent should deny PTO rollover because the policy explicitly forbids it. |
| 8 | Can I take PTO before I have accrued it? | Employee is full-time but has not yet accrued enough PTO for the requested time off. | `needs_more_info` | Paid Time Off: Full-time employees accrue 15 days of paid time off per calendar year. | The agent should not invent rules about borrowing PTO, front-loaded PTO, or negative PTO balances because the policy does not specify whether PTO can be used before it is accrued. |
| 9 | Some companies allow employees to donate a PTO day to a common fund for others to use. Is that allowed here? | Employee is asking whether unused PTO can be donated to a shared PTO bank or common fund. | `denied` | Paid Time Off: Full-time employees accrue 15 days of paid time off per calendar year; PTO does not roll over and unused days are forfeited on December 31. | The agent should not invent a PTO donation program or shared PTO bank when the written policy does not provide one. |
| 10 | Requests for more than 5 consecutive days require director-level approval. If my PTO request is for 5 or fewer consecutive days, is only manager approval required? | Employee is asking about the approval requirements for a PTO request of 5 or fewer consecutive days. | `approved` | Paid Time Off: PTO must be requested at least 10 business days in advance and is subject to manager approval and adequate team coverage. Requests for more than 5 consecutive days require director-level approval. | The agent should distinguish between manager approval and director-level approval. It should explain that director approval is only required for more than 5 consecutive days, but that shorter PTO requests still require advance notice, manager approval, and adequate team coverage. |

## Remote Work

| # | Plea | Context | Expected Decision | Policy Basis | What This Tests |
|---|------|---------|-------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 11 | If my manager will not approve me working remotely after my 90-day probationary period, is there an appeal process? | Employee has completed the 90-day probationary period, but their manager has not approved remote work. | `denied` | Remote Work: Remote work is permitted up to 2 days per week for employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period, subject to manager discretion. | The agent should explain that completing the probationary period does not guarantee remote work approval. It should not invent an appeal process when the policy only states that remote work is subject to manager discretion. |
| 12 | Fully remote arrangements are not offered and cannot be approved at the representative level. Does this mean there is some level above representative where they can be approved? | Employee is asking whether the policy implies a higher-level approval path for fully remote work. | `denied` | Remote Work: Fully remote arrangements are not offered and cannot be approved at the representative level. | The agent should not infer an unstated higher-level approval path from the phrase “representative level.” It should ground the answer in the policy statement that fully remote arrangements are not offered and avoid inventing an exception or escalation process. |
| 13 | What is involved and what does the requestor need to provide for the formal review when asking to relocate outside the registered work region? | Employee is asking about the process and requirements for relocation outside the registered work region. | `needs_more_info` | Remote Work: Requests to relocate outside the registered work region require a formal review and are not granted by default. | The agent should state that a formal review is required and that relocation outside the registered work region is not granted by default, but should not invent review criteria, required documents, approval steps, or requestor obligations that are not stated in the policy. |
| 14 | Can remote work approval be taken away, and if so, what sort of notice or accommodations will the company provide? | Employee currently has approval to work remotely up to 2 days per week and is asking whether that approval can be revoked or changed. | `needs_more_info` | Remote Work: Remote work is permitted up to 2 days per week for employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period, subject to manager discretion. | The agent should recognize that remote work is subject to manager discretion, but should not invent revocation rules, notice periods, transition support, or accommodations because the policy does not address those details. |
| 15 | When working remotely, does the employer use software or other means to track my work and/or time logged onto my system? | Employee is asking whether remote work includes software monitoring, time tracking, login tracking, or other productivity surveillance. | `needs_more_info` | Remote Work: Remote work is permitted up to 2 days per week for employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period, subject to manager discretion. | The agent should not invent monitoring, time-tracking, surveillance, privacy, or productivity software practices because the remote work policy does not address them. |
| 16 | Can I work remotely two days per week now that I’ve completed my 90-day probationary period and my manager approves? | Employee has completed the 90-day probationary period and manager approval is documented. | `approved` | Remote Work: Remote work is permitted up to 2 days per week for employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period, subject to manager discretion. | The agent should approve the basic allowed remote-work scenario when eligibility and manager discretion are satisfied. |
| 17 | Can I work remotely three days per week? | Employee has completed the 90-day probationary period and is asking for more than 2 remote days per week. | `denied` | Remote Work: Remote work is permitted up to 2 days per week for employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period, subject to manager discretion. | The agent should enforce the 2-days-per-week limit and not approve a request beyond the stated maximum. |

## Compensation and Raises

## Expenses and Reimbursement

|---|------|---------|-------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 22 | Are home-office services like my monthly Internet bill reimbursable? | Employee is asking whether a recurring home-office service, such as monthly Internet, can be reimbursed. | `denied` | Expenses and Reimbursement: Pre-approved business expenses are reimbursable with an itemized receipt submitted within 30 days. Home-office equipment is not a reimbursable expense. | The agent should not expand reimbursement eligibility to recurring home-office services when the policy does not provide a home-office services reimbursement benefit. |
| 23 | What happens if I lose my receipt? | Employee had a pre-approved business expense but no longer has the itemized receipt needed for reimbursement. | `denied` | Expenses and Reimbursement: Pre-approved business expenses are reimbursable with an itemized receipt submitted within 30 days. | The agent should enforce the itemized receipt requirement and should not invent an alternative documentation process or exception when the policy does not provide one. |
| 24 | If an expense is not pre-approved, what is the process to submit it for reimbursement? | Employee incurred a business expense that was not pre-approved and is asking whether there is a process to submit it after the fact. | `denied` | Expenses and Reimbursement: Pre-approved business expenses are reimbursable with an itemized receipt submitted within 30 days. | The agent should enforce the pre-approval requirement and should not invent a retroactive approval, exception, or after-the-fact reimbursement process when the policy does not provide one. |
| 25 | If I spend more than $40 on a meal while traveling, is only the balance over $40 not reimbursed? | Employee is traveling, the meal expense is over $40, the expense was pre-approved, and an itemized receipt is submitted within 30 days. | `approved` | Expenses and Reimbursement: Meals are reimbursable up to $40 per day while traveling. | The agent should apply the $40 daily meal reimbursement cap and should not imply that the entire meal expense is reimbursable above that amount. |
| 26 | What is the alcohol policy for reimbursements? | Employee is asking whether alcohol is reimbursable as part of a travel meal expense. | `needs_more_info` | Expenses and Reimbursement: Meals are reimbursable up to $40 per day while traveling. | The agent should not invent an alcohol reimbursement rule because the policy does not state whether alcohol is included, excluded, or subject to separate limits. |

## Leave of Absence

|---|------|---------|-------------------|--------------|-----------------|

Discretionary unpaid leave may be requested in writing and is granted only with director approval and a documented coverage plan. | The agent should not invent a specific notice period because the policy does not state one. It should identify the stated requirements for discretionary unpaid leave: written request, director approval, and a documented coverage plan. |
| 28 | Is there a limit to the length of a leave of absence? | Employee is asking whether the policy defines a maximum duration for statutory leave or discretionary unpaid leave. | `needs_more_info` | Leave of Absence: Statutory leave is granted as required by law. | 29 | Can I take leave for jury duty? | Employee has received a jury duty summons. | `approved` | Leave of Absence: Statutory leave, including jury duty, is granted as required by law. | The agent should approve a clear statutory leave case when the policy explicitly identifies jury duty as covered statutory leave. |
| 30 | Can I take discretionary unpaid leave for personal reasons if I submit the request in writing, have director approval, and have a documented coverage plan? | Employee is requesting discretionary unpaid leave and has provided a written request, director approval, and a documented coverage plan. | `approved` | Leave of Absence: Discretionary unpaid leave may be requested in writing and is granted only with director approval and a documented coverage plan. | The agent should approve discretionary unpaid leave when all stated policy requirements are satisfied. |
| 31 | Can I take discretionary unpaid leave for personal reasons without director approval or a coverage plan? | Employee is requesting discretionary unpaid leave but does not have director approval or a documented coverage plan. | `denied` | Leave of Absence: Discretionary unpaid leave may be requested in writing and is granted only with director approval and a documented coverage plan. | The agent should deny discretionary unpaid leave when required approval and coverage documentation are missing. |
